<objective>
Close the atomic-consumption loop. Extend `completeTaskAction` (the only writer of completions in the codebase) to batch `completions.create` + `schedule_overrides.update({consumed_at: now})` in one `pb.createBatch()` transaction. Without this plan, a snoozed-and-completed task could show "perma-snoozed" on next render (covered defensively by the D-10 read-time filter in Plan 10-02's `computeNextDue`, but write-time consumption is the primary contract).

Purpose: Make SNZE-06 green — "Overrides are consumed when the next completion lands after the override date." Atomicity ensures no observable race where the completion lands but the override stays active.
Output: 1 modified server action + 1 new integration-test scenario + documented PB batch-result shape.
</objective>

## Trust Boundaries

| Boundary | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| completion write → PB | User-initiated completion action mutates two collections (completions + schedule_overrides) in a single transaction |
| TOCTOU window: getActiveOverride → batch.send() | Time-of-check vs. time-of-use: the fetch happens at T0; the batch sends at T0+Δms; during Δ, a concurrent writer could consume or delete the override |
